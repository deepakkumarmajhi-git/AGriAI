# SmartAgri Revenue Model (B2B2F)

## 1) Positioning
SmartAgri runs a hybrid **B2B2F** model:
- Core product remains free for farmers to maximize trust and adoption.
- Revenue is generated from ecosystem participants who get measurable business value.
- Premium farmer subscription is delayed until retention and usage are stable.

## 2) Monetization Architecture

## 2.1 Marketplace and Waste Commission (Primary)
- Scope: closed produce and agri-waste transactions initiated in SmartAgri.
- Take rate:
  - Produce: **2% to 3%**
  - Waste/biomass: **3% to 4%**
- Initial operating rule:
  - Charge commission only on successful fulfillment.
  - Keep payout cycle weekly to build seller trust.
- Rationale: directly tied to farmer outcome and GMV growth.

## 2.2 Embedded Partner Lead Fees (Primary)
- Scope: qualified leads for agri-inputs, crop insurance, agri-loans, and scheme facilitation partners.
- Fee band: **INR 40 to INR 150 per qualified lead**.
- Lead quality definition:
  - Farmer identity validated
  - Product/partner intent captured
  - Contactability confirmed
- Commercial model:
  - Per qualified lead fee
  - Optional bonus for approved/converted leads (partner-specific)

## 2.3 Institutional Subscription (Primary)
- Buyers: FPOs, co-ops, NGOs, CSR programs, and agri program operators.
- Pricing:
  - Starter: **INR 7,500/month** up to 300 farmers
  - Growth: **INR 20,000/month** up to 1,500 farmers
  - Enterprise: custom SLA + onboarding fee
- Includes:
  - cohort dashboards
  - advisory rollups
  - usage analytics
  - coordinator/admin access

## 2.4 Farmer Pro (Secondary, delayed launch)
- Price:
  - **INR 99/month**
  - **INR 899/year**
- Proposed features:
  - advanced scan history insights
  - unlimited or higher AI usage limits
  - priority assistant response
  - advanced crop reports
- Launch gate:
  - weekly retention stable for 8+ weeks
  - support load within SLA
  - clear value deltas validated by pilot users

## 3) Who Pays
- Farmers: Free (default), optional Pro later
- Buyers/recyclers: commission on closed transactions
- Banks/insurers/input firms: lead fees
- FPO/NGO/program operators: subscription

## 4) 12-Month Revenue Mix Target
- 40% marketplace + waste commissions
- 35% institutional subscriptions
- 20% partner lead fees
- 5% farmer Pro subscriptions

## 5) Go-to-Market Execution Sequence

## Phase 1 (Months 0-3)
- Launch monetization in 2 pilot districts.
- Activate commissions on marketplace and waste exchange.
- Sign 3 to 5 lead-buying partners.
- Target: first recurring institutional customer.

## Phase 2 (Months 3-6)
- Scale FPO/NGO subscriptions.
- Add partner performance reporting cadence.
- Expand district footprint and onboarding operations.
- Target: monthly GMV >= INR 25 lakh by month 6.

## Phase 3 (Months 6-12)
- Launch Farmer Pro to high-usage cohorts only.
- Add annual institutional contracts and partner bundles.
- Optimize CAC and partner conversion economics.

## 6) Unit Economics Model

## 6.1 Core formulas
- `Commission Revenue = GMV * Take Rate`
- `Lead Revenue = Qualified Leads * Avg Lead Fee`
- `Subscription Revenue = Active Accounts * ARPA`
- `Blended Revenue = Commission + Lead + Subscription + Pro`
- `LTV/CAC = Customer Lifetime Value / Customer Acquisition Cost`

## 6.2 Minimum viability thresholds
- Lead acceptance rate >= 35%
- Institutional monthly churn < 8%
- Pro conversion >= 3% of MAU post-launch
- LTV/CAC > 3 by year-end

## 7) Operating Cadence
- Weekly:
  - GMV review by district
  - lead quality and partner acceptance review
  - support incidents and farmer trust signals
- Monthly:
  - revenue mix vs target
  - cohort retention
  - churn and reactivation
  - district expansion decision
- Quarterly:
  - pricing recalibration
  - partner contract renewal and upsell
  - product gating review for Pro

## 8) Risks and Mitigation
- Low transaction closure:
  - Mitigation: better buyer discovery and listing quality standards.
- Partner distrust in lead quality:
  - Mitigation: strict qualification criteria and transparent lead scoring.
- Institutional churn:
  - Mitigation: dedicated onboarding, usage nudges, and quarterly value reports.
- Early Pro push hurting trust:
  - Mitigation: keep core free, launch Pro only after retention gate passes.

## 9) KPI Scorecard (Decision Metrics)
- GMV (produce and waste separately)
- Effective commission rate
- Qualified leads and acceptance rate
- Active paid institutions and ARPA
- MAU, WAU, and retention cohort curves
- Pro conversion rate (post-launch)
- Customer support SLA and resolution time
- LTV/CAC

## 10) Ownership Model (Suggested)
- Business owner: Founder/CEO
- Revenue operations owner: GTM lead
- Institutional sales owner: Partnerships manager
- Partner lead owner: Business development manager
- Metrics owner: Product ops/analytics

## 11) Current Scope Boundary
- This plan requires **no product API or code changes** for definition.
- Billing automation, payout rails, and detailed metering can be added in a later implementation phase.
